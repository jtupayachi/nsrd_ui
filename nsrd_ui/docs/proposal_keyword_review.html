[ADD: “This project is structured as a Seed Project under the Lighthouse Challenge framework, pressure-testing AI-driven workflows on well-characterized EM data before broader deployment.”]

Proposed Research and Methods

The 9-month (M) effort will establish a proof-of-concept AI-guided data and knowledge workflow for the EM environmental data use case. We will perform three tasks.

Task 1. AI-Guided Data Curation and Harmonization (M1 – M4)

Task 1 directly supports the [REPLACE: “Data Curation component of the AI R&D Roadmap” → “Data Curation component of the EM AI R&D Roadmap Implementation (Focus Area A)”] and establishes the quality benchmarks and monitoring foundations needed for later deployment and improvement. [ADD: “This task will enhance data accessibility across EM sites by converting historical data into standardized formats and evaluating multimodal data assets for AI applications, directly addressing the roadmap’s data-readiness goals.”]

Task 1.1. AI-guided data discovery and organization. We will identify and organize historical data assets across three source tiers: (1) expert-curated foundational datasets, including NEPA Environmental Impact Statements (EIS) and associated EPA EIS database records, EM cleanup site operation data from ORCC, tabular environmental and operational datasets, and other authoritative historical records; (2) expert-selected literature and knowledge sources, including books, journal articles, and technical reports; and (3) MCP-enabled retrieval and crawl data used to supplement and update the knowledge base. For each source, AI agents will assist with query generation, document retrieval, source classification, duplicate detection, and relevance screening against predefined EM decision questions and priority variables. [ADD: “Data assets will include operating & administrative procedures, characterization records spanning fractured systems, subsurface contamination, and liquid waste, as well as environmental liability and financial forecasts.”] Because crawl data may be less reliable than expert-curated and operational sources, it will be assigned lower source-confidence scores and routed through provenance review and expert validation before incorporation. An AI-guided workflow will be further developed to support source discovery, document screening, and initial relevance assessment based on our recent success in Automating Geospatial Web Dashboards Using Large Language Models and AI Agents for Decision Support.5 The primary outcome will be a prioritized inventory of candidate data assets, including source type, relevance score, confidence level, key variables, spatial/temporal coverage, operational relevance, and downstream use cases.

Task 1.2. Multimodal data harmonization and repository development. Using the prioritized sources identified in Task 1.1, we will construct a harmonized, traceable, and extensible multimodal repository that links structured environmental and operational datasets with unstructured historical and technical records. [ADD: “This repository will serve as the data foundation for a Scale-Bridging AI Foundation Model (Focus Area B) that predicts scale-dependent behavior across lab, pilot, and full-scale systems.”] The initial air-domain repository will build on an existing ORR meteorological dataset containing structured measurements of temperature, pressure, humidity, wind, precipitation, and solar radiation. We will extend this site-based air dataset into an [REPLACE: “AI-ready multimodal repository” → “AI-Ready Dataset repository”] by linking structured monitoring data with document-derived context from EIS reports, agency records, technical reports, literature, and MCP-retrieved updates. Structured datasets will be harmonized by variable, unit, location, timestamp, and reporting period, while unstructured documents will be parsed into retrieval-ready segments and annotated with metadata, including source, document type, site/location, date, environmental medium, key variables, and decision context. A common harmonization schema will align records across variables, locations, time windows, document types, and provenance fields. Shared identifiers will connect air-domain measurements with relevant document segments, regulatory records, and technical knowledge. After completing the air-domain repository, we will assess candidate soil and water datasets based on data quality, quantity, metadata completeness, spatial-temporal coverage, and relevance to EM decision questions. Selected soil or water datasets will then be incorporated into the same Task 1 harmonization workflow to support cross-domain validation in Task 2.

Task 1.3. Data quality benchmarking, cataloging, and readiness assessment. To support [REPLACE: “AI-ready data preparation” → “AI-Ready Dataset preparation”], we will implement a lightweight quality engine for data cleaning, validation, deduplication, source-confidence assessment, and schema conformance during ingestion. Quality checks will assess completeness, spatial consistency, temporal coherence, provenance confidence, unit consistency, duplication, and cross-source linkage quality. Machine-readable metadata and cataloging will support discoverability, lineage tracing, retrieval readiness, and future incorporation of additional data from users and partners through common metadata and schema standards. [ADD: “The quality engine will also support CARS analyses and best contract mechanisms by providing verifiable data lineage and quality metrics.”] For the air-domain foundation, the prior ORR meteorological dataset already provides a quality-controlled starting point. This project will build on those quality-control procedures and extend them into a broader AI-readiness framework that evaluates both structured datasets and document-derived knowledge sources. For soil and water datasets, readiness assessment will be used as a selection and screening step to determine whether the data are sufficient for harmonization, retrieval, analytics, and cross-domain testing. The overall outcome of Task 1 will be a harmonized, traceable, and knowledge-ready multimodal data repository, beginning with an AI-enhanced air-domain foundation and extending to selected soil and/or water datasets as data quality and availability allow. This repository, together with metadata, quality benchmarks, and readiness indicators, will provide the data-curation foundation for downstream retrieval, synthesis, analytics, deployment, and continuous-improvement workflows under the EM AI R&D Roadmap.

Task 2. AI-Enhanced EM Analytics (M1 – M6)

Task 2 will build an analytics workflow for transforming the [REPLACE: “AI-ready repository” → “AI-Ready Dataset repository”] developed in Task 1 into [REPLACE: “adaptive monitoring and early-warning support” → “adaptive & real-time monitoring and early warning systems for contamination and critical infrastructure failures”] for EM decision-making.

Task 2.1 Air-domain adaptive monitoring and early-warning analytics. Using the AI-ready repository developed in Task 1, we will develop an air-domain analytics workflow that builds on our prior EDA and machine-learning analyses of ORR air-domain data. This analytics workflow will include EDA to characterize temporal trends, spatial patterns, correlations among key air-related variables, and clustering patterns associated with extreme weather conditions. It will also include machine-learning models for predictive analytics, including forecasting selected air-quality indicators, identifying high-risk periods or locations, and evaluating variable importance. The workflow will support [REPLACE: “adaptive and near-real-time monitoring concepts for early warning of contamination events and critical infrastructure or site-operation failures” → “adaptive & real-time monitoring for early warning systems for contamination and critical infrastructure failures, supporting integrated performance and risk assessment and risk-informed decision analyses”]. [ADD: “Analytics outputs will also support optimization of waste treatment processes, remedial systems, waste disposition, and site operations, directly contributing to Treatment Process Optimization (Focus Area C) at EM sites such as SRS and Hanford.”]

Task 2.2 Cross-domain analytics validation using water/soil data. To evaluate whether the air-domain analytics workflow can be rapidly adapted to other EM-relevant environmental media, we will apply the workflow to representative water or soil datasets selected in Task 1. Domain-specific variables, such as contaminant concentration, sampling depth, environmental medium, sampling method, location, and reporting period, will be mapped to the shared schema and analyzed using the same evaluation framework. We will compare results across air and water/soil to identify reusable workflow components, domain-specific adjustments, and modifications needed for Phase II scale-up. [ADD: “Validation will also test the framework’s ability to support search for critical isotopes and characterization of fractured systems, subsurface contamination, and liquid waste across different environmental media.”] This task will test whether the common schema, analytics-oriented feature construction, EDA procedures, and predictive modeling framework can be extended beyond the initial air-domain use case. Validation will assess whether the workflow can align records across locations and time periods, identify patterns and trends, support initial predictive analysis, and generate traceable outputs for EM monitoring and decision support in additional environmental media. [ADD: “Cross-domain results will inform environmental liability and financial forecasts by providing validated predictive capabilities across multiple environmental media.”] The outcome will be a validation summary documenting reusable components, domain-specific requirements, model-validation results, and recommendations for rapid deployment across additional EM sites and data domains in Phase II.

The overall outcome of Task 2 will be an AI-enhanced environmental monitoring and analytics workflow that transforms the AI-ready repository from Task 1 into interpretable and traceable support for EM decision-making. The workflow will support EM cleanup site operations, including environmental monitoring, remediation, nuclear fuel and waste management, [REPLACE: “waste disposal, storage, transport” → “waste disposal, storage, transport, waste disposition”], and related mission activities. Task 2 results will provide the validated analytics foundation for the Task 3 human-in-the-loop decision-making framework.
