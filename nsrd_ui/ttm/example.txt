Commercializing ORNL's DT Cryogenic Pellet Injection System
Opportunity / Technology Description
Problem addressed: Reliable DT fueling of burning plasmas, pacing/remediating edge-localized modes, and disruption mitigation in magnetic-confinement devices.
Technical description: Tritium-compatible twin-screw extruder feeding a dual-nozzle cutter (5 mm fueling; 3 mm ELM pacing), a gas-gun accelerator with HFS/LFS guide tubes, plus pipe-gun shattered pellet injection (SPI) for disruption mitigation. Key ITER specs include pellet sizes, repetition rates, and per-injector fueling throughput.
Invention disclosures:
2024056875, Method for Cryogenic Extrusion Recirculation
202506044, Tritium-Compatible Cryogenic Screw
202506047, Tritium-Compatible Cryogenic Pellet Gas Gun
202506052, Pressure Relief Device Cryogenic Fusion Fuel
Development stage: Laboratory prototypes demonstrated; ITER-scale components designed and under test with US ITER support.
Fundamental advantage: Efficient core fueling; lower divertor heat flux; a collimated spray enabling fast quench compared with MGI.
Market Opportunity and Expected Impact
Who has the problem now: ITER and private fusion developers scaling demo/pilot plants.
Current solutions & gap: Gas puff/NBI have fueling limitations; need tritium-compatible continuous extrusion (dual-diameter pellets) and SPI meeting <20 ms delivery windows from port-cell locations for rapid quench.
Quantity (technical data):
Per-injector fueling throughput up to 120 Pa·m³/s
5 mm fueling pellets (50–92 mm)
3 mm ELM pellets (17–33 mm)
≈4 Hz vs 16 Hz repetition
SPI 300–450 m/s fragment speed
30°–40° spray cone
Applications (rough order):
ITER fueling/ELM/SPI subsystems
Private pilot plants
Research devices needing impurity/fueling injectors
Licensing pathway recommendation: Staged option-to-license coupled to CRADA/SPP milestones (avoid broad exclusivity until proven partnership; consider field-limited exclusivity for fueling systems) for a component supplier.
Go-to-Market Plan and Technical Development Plan
Months 1–3 (partner discovery)
Target fusion developers (tokamak, stellarator, mirror), US ITER, and qualified component suppliers.
Publish technology listing.
NDA-protected technical webinar aligned with DOE domestic manufacturing determinations for licensing.
Months 2–11 (technical validation)
Validate twin-screw extruder throughput (≥0.3 g/s DT equivalent) under SCHe cooling.
Demonstrate dual-nozzle cutter repeatability; verify pellet survivability through ITER-relevant bends (≈850 mm).
Execute SPI formation/shatter tests (25–34 mm neon/D₂), characterize spray geometry.
Prepare safety basis (inventory limits, propellant recirculation), QA, and export control.
Expected outcomes for commercial interest:
A partner-ready data package with stable extrusion/cutting, repetition rates (fueling ≤4 Hz, ELM ≤16 Hz), validated spray geometry, and subsystem integration demonstrations.
Key risks:
Cryogenic throughput stability; tritium compatibility and safety/regulatory requirements; survivability/fragmentation control; integration differences across reactor geometries; long fusion timelines with few near-term customers.
Value Proposition and Deployment Plan
Alternatives: Gas puff fueling, neutral-beam fueling, legacy single-screw pellet systems.
Why better:
HFS pellet fueling increases core fueling efficiency.
ELM pacing reduces load per event.
SPI delivers faster, more tightly coupled mass injection than MGI during disruptions.
Integration challenges: Medium–High (tritium safety, SCHe cooling, propellant recirculation, remote handling).
Private-sector partners: Qualified cryogenic and valve/injector suppliers; private fusion firms; US ITER collaborators. Best near-term license holder: an established component supplier with sublicensing rights to reactor OEMs.
End users/customers: ITER research program, private fusion pilot plants, and national/international fusion facilities.
